Top 10 budget international destinations for Indians

A practical 2026 guide to the cheapest countries you can fly to from India — ranked by total trip cost, visa friction, safety, and the experience-per-rupee you actually get.

Profile: First-time or seasoned Indian traveller Target trip: 7–10 days, mid-range comfort Budget: ₹40K–₹90K all-in (flight + stay + food)

The 30-second answer

  • Cheapest overall: Nepal & Sri Lanka — sub-₹35K all-in for a week. No visa required for Nepal; ETA-only for Sri Lanka. Both border India, so flights are cheap year-round.
  • Best value-for-rupee: Vietnam & Thailand — under ₹50K for 7 days including flight. E-visa for Vietnam, visa-free for Indians to Thailand (active 2024 onward, check current status).
  • Most underrated: Georgia & Azerbaijan — European feel at Southeast Asian prices. E-visa, beautiful mountains, cheap wine, ₹50–60K for a week.
  • Skip Bali in 2026 unless you really want it — visa-on-arrival fees + flight cost has pushed it past ₹70K, and Vietnam delivers a similar tropical experience for half the price.

02Top 3 deep dives

If you're picking just one trip this year, these three give you the best experience-per-rupee ratio.

🇳🇵 Nepal — the cheapest passport-stamp you'll ever get

Line itemCost (INR)
Round-trip flight Delhi → Kathmandu₹10,000–14,000
VisaFREE (Indians don't need one)
Accommodation · 6 nights (mid hostel/guesthouse)₹4,800
Food (3 meals/day, mix of local + tourist)₹4,200
Inter-city travel (bus to Pokhara + return)₹2,000
Activities (Phewa lake boat, paragliding, temples)₹3,500
Total₹24,500–28,500

Why go

  • No visa, no SIM hassle (Indian SIM works in border areas), INR accepted almost everywhere
  • Annapurna circuit, EBC base treks for fit travellers — world-class hiking at backpacker prices
  • Pokhara is a chill 2-day stop with lake, paragliding, and bakeries
  • Cheap domestic flights inside Nepal (KTM → Pokhara is ₹4–5K and 25 min)

Watch out for

  • Air pollution in Kathmandu Nov–Feb is genuinely bad — N95 mask recommended
  • Monsoon (Jun–Sep) = bad mountain views, landslides on roads
  • Earthquake risk is real; pick well-built hotels in tourist areas
  • Touristy areas like Thamel can feel like Goa-on-steroids — leave the bubble to see real Nepal

🇻🇳 Vietnam — the SE Asia trip everyone underrates

Line itemCost (INR)
Round-trip flight Delhi/Mumbai → Hanoi or Saigon₹20,000–26,000
E-visa (90-day, multiple-entry)~₹2,200 ($25)
Accommodation · 6 nights (hostel/budget hotel)₹6,000
Food (incredible street food, $2–4 meals)₹4,500
Domestic travel (sleeper bus + 1 internal flight)₹4,500
Activities (Halong cruise, Hoi An lantern boat, Cu Chi tunnels)₹6,500
Total₹43,700–49,700

Why go

  • Street food culture rivals Bangkok — pho, banh mi, bun cha, ca phe sua da
  • Three very different regions in one country: Halong Bay (north), Hoi An old town (centre), Saigon (south)
  • Excellent backpacker infrastructure — Sapa, Da Nang, Phong Nha caves all easy to reach
  • One of the safest SE Asian countries for solo travellers

Watch out for

  • North to south is a long country — pick 2 regions max for a 7-day trip
  • Crossing roads in Hanoi/Saigon is a real skill (just walk slowly and don't stop)
  • Taxi scams at airports — use Grab app instead
  • Veg food exists but isn't as easy as Thailand — pure-veg travellers should plan

  • European vibe (cobbled old town, churches, wine country) without Schengen visa pain
  • One of the world's oldest wine traditions — vineyards in Kakheti for ₹500/tasting
  • Kazbegi day-trip rivals any Alps view at a fraction of the cost
  • Very Indian-friendly — large Indian student population in Tbilisi means familiar food easy to find

  • Winters (Dec–Feb) are cold and many mountain roads close

04The final ranking

Based on cost, ease, safety, and experience-per-rupee for an Indian traveller in 2026.

1

Nepal — for your first international trip ever

Nothing else comes close on price. No visa, INR works, flights are cheap year-round, and you get genuine mountains. If you've never been abroad, start here — passport stamp, low risk, ₹25K out the door.

2

Vietnam — for the best 10-day budget trip on this list

If you've done Nepal/Sri Lanka and want a real "SE Asia trip" with food, culture, and varied landscapes, Vietnam beats Thailand on both price and authenticity. Hanoi → Halong → Hoi An is the perfect 10-day route.

3

Georgia — for the most underrated trip

Cobblestone old town, wine country, snow-capped mountains, and the cheapest "European feeling" trip an Indian can take. Use it as a Schengen visa rehearsal — Georgia is great visa practice and stamps look great on a passport.

4

Sri Lanka — for families & relaxed beach trips

English everywhere, sane prices, beaches + tea country + Sigiriya in 7 days. Best "second international" trip after Nepal. Don't go May–Sep (monsoon on the west/south coast).

5

Thailand — for the classic, well-trodden first SE Asia

Visa-free, easy, predictable. Bangkok + Krabi/Phuket = the safest, most-organised first SE Asia trip. Slightly more expensive than Vietnam now, but easier for nervous travellers.

05Plan-your-trip timeline

From "I want to go" to "boarding pass on phone" — what to do, in order.

12 weeks out
Pick the country, lock dates. Use Google Flights "Explore" view to spot the cheapest week. Cross-check with the destination's "best season" — avoid monsoons.
10 weeks out
Book the flight. Indian → SE Asia or Caucasus sweet spot is 8–10 weeks out. Use Skyscanner + ixigo + airline direct. Set Hopper-style price alerts.
8 weeks out
Apply for visa. E-visas take 3–7 working days. Don't leave it for 2 weeks before — Vietnam and Cambodia e-visa portals are notoriously slow.
6 weeks out
Book accommodation. Hostelworld for hostels, Agoda for hotels (often cheaper than Booking in Asia). Book the first 2 nights firmly, keep the rest flexible.
4 weeks out
Get the right credit/debit card. Get a 0% forex-markup card (IDFC, ICICI Coral) and a backup. Carry $200–300 in cash for emergencies. Inform your bank you're travelling.
2 weeks out
Travel insurance + eSIM. ICICI/Bajaj travel insurance for ₹500–800 for 7 days. Buy an Airalo or Holafly eSIM (₹500–800) — much cheaper than international roaming.
3 days out
Final checklist. Passport (6+ months validity), printed visa, hotel address printed (immigration may ask), Indian credit card with ₹50K+ limit, paracetamol/Imodium/ORS.

06Money-saving hacks that actually work

  1. Tuesday/Wednesday flights are 15–25% cheaper. Friday-Sunday flights from Indian metros are most expensive. Shift dates by 2 days and save ₹4–6K easily.
  2. Don't exchange INR at airports. Worst rates in the world. Use a 0% forex card at ATMs in the destination (₹200–300 ATM fee, but rate is mid-market).
  3. Skip hotel breakfast. Local breakfast costs ₹100–200; hotel buffet adds ₹600–1,200/day to your stay. Eat where locals eat.
  4. Use the Wise card or IDFC First Wealth. Both give near-bank exchange rates with no markup. Wise multi-currency account is gold for frequent travellers.
  5. Buy day-tours from local agencies, not online. Booking a Halong cruise from a Hanoi street agency is 30–40% cheaper than GetYourGuide.
  6. Public transit + ride-hail apps. Grab (SE Asia), Bolt (Georgia, Africa), Yandex (Central Asia, ME). Never take "taxi" from outside an airport without a price agreed up front.
